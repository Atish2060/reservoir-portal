Upper Tamakoshi Hydropower

About Upper Tamakoshi Hydropower

The Upper Tamakoshi Hydroelectric Project is Nepal's largest hydropower plant, located in the Gaurishankar Conservation Area of Dolakha District. This run-of-the-river project has an installed capacity of 456 MW and began operations in 2021. It plays a crucial role in meeting Nepal's growing energy demands by generating approximately 2,281 GWh of electricity annually.

The project features a 22-meter-high and 60-meter-long diversion dam at Lamabagar, channeling water through an 8.4 km headrace tunnel to an underground powerhouse equipped with six Pelton turbines. The design utilizes a gross head of 822 meters and a design flow of 66 m³/s to maximize energy production.

Entirely financed by domestic institutions, the Upper Tamakoshi project stands as a testament to Nepal's commitment to sustainable energy development. It significantly reduces the nation's dependence on imported electricity, contributing to energy security and economic growth.
