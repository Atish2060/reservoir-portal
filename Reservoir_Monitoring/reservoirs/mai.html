Mai Hydropower Project

About Mai Hydropower

The Mai Hydropower Project is located in Ilam District, Nepal. It is a run-of-river type project developed to harness the potential of the Mai River for electricity generation.

The plant has an installed capacity of 22 MW and plays a crucial role in supplying power to the eastern region of Nepal. The project contributes to the national grid and supports Nepal’s push for sustainable energy production.

The project includes a diversion weir, headrace tunnel, and underground powerhouse with Francis turbines. Its efficient design ensures year-round power generation, benefiting local communities and industries.
