Kaligandaki Hydropower

About Kaligandaki Hydropower

The Kaligandaki A Hydroelectric Power Station, located near Mirmi in Syangja District, is Nepal's largest hydropower plant with an installed capacity of 144 MW. Commissioned in 2002, it significantly contributes to the nation's electricity supply.

This run-of-river project utilizes the flow of the Kaligandaki River, featuring a 44-meter-high concrete gravity dam and a 5.9 km headrace tunnel leading to the powerhouse equipped with three 48 MW Francis turbine-generators.

The project plays a vital role in meeting Nepal's energy demands, providing approximately 860 GWh of electricity annually, and supports economic growth by supplying power to major cities like Kathmandu and Pokhara.

Additionally, the project has implemented sediment management strategies to address the high sediment load of the Kaligandaki River, ensuring sustainable operation and longevity of the facility.
