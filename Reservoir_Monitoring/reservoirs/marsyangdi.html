Marsyangdi Hydropower Station

About Marsyangdi Hydropower Station

The Marsyangdi Hydropower Station is a run-of-river hydroelectric plant located in Aanbukhaireni, Tanahu District, Nepal. Commissioned in 1989, it has an installed capacity of 69 MW, generated by three units of 23 MW each. The plant harnesses the flow of the Marsyangdi River to produce approximately 462.5 GWh of electricity annually, contributing significantly to Nepal's power supply.

The project was developed with assistance from various international agencies, including the International Development Association (IDA), KfW, Kuwait Fund for Arab Economic Development (KFED), Saudi Fund for Development (SFD), and the Asian Development Bank (ADB), at a cost of USD 22 million.
