Hydropower Generation in Nepal

Hydropower is a cornerstone of Nepal's energy strategy, leveraging the country's abundant rivers and mountainous terrain. With a potential capacity of over 83,000 MW, Nepal is poised to become a leader in renewable energy in South Asia.

Current Projects

  • Upper Tamakoshi Hydropower Project:
    • Capacity: 456 MW
    • Location: Dolakha District
    • Status: Operational since 2021
    • Key Feature: Largest hydropower project fully funded by Nepali investors
  • Gandaki Hydropower Project:
    • Capacity: 500 MW
    • Location: Gandaki River Basin
    • Status: Under construction (Expected completion: 2025)
    • Key Feature: Focus on eco-friendly construction and community benefits
  • Kulekhani Reservoir:
    • Capacity: 92 MW
    • Location: Makwanpur District
    • Status: Operational since 1982
    • Key Feature: First reservoir-based hydropower project in Nepal
  • Arun III Hydropower Project:
    • Capacity: 900 MW
    • Location: Sankhuwasabha District
    • Status: Under construction (Expected completion: 2024)
    • Key Feature: Developed in collaboration with India's Satluj Jal Vidyut Nigam (SJVN)
  • Upper Karnali Hydropower Project:
    • Capacity: 900 MW
    • Location: Surkhet District
    • Status: Under development
    • Key Feature: Export-oriented project with power supply to India and Bangladesh
  • Budhi Gandaki Hydropower Project:
    • Capacity: 1,200 MW
    • Location: Gorkha and Dhading Districts
    • Status: Planned
    • Key Feature: Largest hydropower project in Nepal, expected to be a game-changer for the energy sector

Environmental and Social Impact

Our hydropower projects are designed with a focus on sustainability and community benefits. Key initiatives include:

  • Minimizing environmental impact through eco-friendly construction
  • Providing clean and reliable energy to rural areas
  • Creating jobs and supporting local economies

Future Plans

The government aims to increase hydropower generation to 10,000 MW by 2030, with a focus on public-private partnerships and international collaboration. Key future projects include:

  • West Seti Hydropower Project: 750 MW (Far Western Region)
  • Tamor Hydropower Project: 762 MW (Eastern Region)
  • Nalsing Gad Hydropower Project: 410 MW (Western Region)
